About Me

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Mathematics at the University of Alabama, working under the supervision of Prof. Shan Zhao and Prof. Yuanzhen Shao. Understanding how complex systems operate has always been my driving force. To explore the mechanics of the economy, I pursued a B.Sc. in Mathematics with Economics at the University of Cape Coast. Following my undergraduate studies, I worked for two years as a Sales Operations Analyst at FanMilk Plc in Accra (a subsidiary of the French conglomerate Danone S.A.), which is one of the largest and best-performing companies listed on the Ghana Stock Exchange. During this time, my desire to deeply understand the regional financial system led me to obtain a securities certification from the exchange.

I subsequently earned a Master's degree in Mathematics from AIMS Ghana, followed by a graduate certificate in Business Management from the European School of Management and Technology (ESMT) Berlin. My AIMS thesis, supervised by Prof. Farai Nyabadza of the University of Johannesburg, focused on "Modeling the Role of Imitation on Crime Dynamics." Up to that point, my primary goal was to understand economic and social systems to create meaningful solutions to societal challenges. However, the COVID-19 pandemic profoundly shifted my perspective, inspiring me to explore how mathematics can be applied to drug design.

This realization led me to my current Ph.D. studies. Today, my work sits at the intersection of applied mathematics, numerical analysis, scientific computing, and mathematical biology. Specifically, my research focuses on:

  • Algorithm Development: Developing and analyzing fast, robust numerical algorithms for solving complex ordinary and partial differential equations.
  • Computational Biophysics: Applying these numerical schemes to problems in molecular biology, with a particular emphasis on solvation energy calculations and binding analysis.
  • Social Dynamics Modeling: Leveraging mathematical epidemiology to study and model complex social phenomena, such as the spread of crime and gang activity.
