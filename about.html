About Me

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Mathematics at the University of Alabama, working under the supervision of Prof. Shan Zhao and Prof. Yuanzhen Shao. My doctoral research focuses on developing fast, robust numerical algorithms for partial differential equations (PDEs), with applications in computational biophysics (biomolecular solvation) and social dynamics modeling.

Previously, I earned a B.Sc. in Mathematics with Economics from the University of Cape Coast, an M.Sc. in Mathematical Sciences from AIMS Ghana, and a graduate certificate in Business Management from ESMT Berlin, alongside working as a Sales Operations Analyst at FanMilk Plc.

Specifically, my current research focuses on:

  • Algorithm Development: Developing and analyzing fast, robust numerical algorithms for solving complex ordinary and partial differential equations.
  • Computational Biophysics: Applying these numerical schemes to problems in molecular biology, with a particular emphasis on solvation energy calculations and binding analysis.
  • Social Dynamics Modeling: Leveraging mathematical epidemiology to study and model complex social phenomena, such as the spread of crime and gang activity.
